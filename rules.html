Trading Rules
Based on ICT concepts & Dodgy's iFVG Ultimate+ model. These rules exist to protect your capital and keep you consistent.
Pre-Trade
01
Sessions
Trade all sessions, with primary focus on London and NY AM. Other sessions are permitted but treated with extra scrutiny.
Required
02
Top Down Analysis
Must complete top-down analysis from Daily → 4H → 1H → 15m → 5m before any entry. Identify HTF bias, key PDA arrays, and liquidity targets before the session opens.
  • Never take a trade that goes against your HTF bias
  • If bias is bearish, only look for shorts
  • If bias is bullish, only look for longs
  • No exceptions
Required
Entry
03
Entry Model
Primary entry is any PDA array (IFVG, FVG, Order Block, Breaker Block, Mitigation Block). Preferred entry is IFVG or FVG for tightest stop placement.
Required
04
Liquidity Sweep
A BSL or SSL sweep before entry is preferred but not required. If no sweep has occurred, confluence must be stronger to justify the entry.
Preferred
05
Stop Loss Placement
  • Preferred: Above/below the IFVG or FVG for the tightest stop
  • Alternative: Above/below the nearest swing high/low, only if close enough to keep risk acceptable. If the swing is too far, revert to IFVG/FVG or skip the trade
  • Never take a trade where no tight stop option exists
Required
06
Clear Target
Having a clear liquidity or PDA target before entry is preferred. Avoid entering into a void with no obvious target.
Preferred
Trade Management
07
Take Profit & Exit Management
  • If a significant high/low exists: Take partial at TP1, trail the remainder
  • If no significant level: Full exit at the liquidity target or PDA
  • Do not move TP once set unless price gives a clear reason
Required
08
Scaling In
A second contract may be added on A+ setups when price retraces into a PDA array. Do not scale in on B-grade or lower setups.
Preferred
09
News Events
Avoid trading during high impact news if the setup is weak (B grade or lower). A+ setups may still be taken but with caution and tighter management.
Preferred
Risk & Psychology
10
Max Loss Per Trade / Day
  • Max risk per trade: 1–2% of account
  • Stop trading for the day after 2 consecutive losses
Required
11
When To Stop Trading
Stop for the day when any of the following are met:
  • 2 consecutive losses hit
  • 1 big win achieved
  • 2–3 good wins achieved
Do not chase after stopping.
Required
12
Mental State & Discipline
  • Do not trade when emotional, frustrated, or sleep-deprived — work in progress
  • No revenge trading after a loss
  • No FOMO entries — if the move is missed, wait for the next setup
  • Do not overtrade — max 2–4 trades per day
Work In Progress
Accountability
13
Journaling
Every trade must be journaled on the same day it is taken. No exceptions.
Required
