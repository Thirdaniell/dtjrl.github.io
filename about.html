Setup Grading System

Every trade is graded based on ICT principles and Dodgy's iFVG Ultimate+ model. Grades reflect the quality of the setup — not the outcome. A D-grade trade can still win, and an A++ can still lose. The goal is to only be taking A and above setups consistently.

A++
Elite Setup
The highest quality setup possible. Every major confluence aligns perfectly. HTF bias confirmed, liquidity swept, IFVG/FVG entry, MSS/CHoCH confirmed, clear PDA target, inside a kill zone, SMT divergence present. These are rare — if you see one, take it without hesitation.
HTF Bias Confirmed Liquidity Swept IFVG or FVG Entry MSS or CHoCH Clear PDA Target Kill Zone SMT Divergence HTF PDA Delivery
A+
Strong Setup
High conviction setup. Most major confluences present. HTF bias aligned, IFVG/FVG or strong OB entry, clear target visible, likely in or near a kill zone. Missing 1 major confluence (e.g. no SMT or no explicit liquidity sweep) but overall a high quality trade worth taking.
HTF Bias Confirmed IFVG/FVG or OB Entry Clear Target 4-5 Confluences
A
Good Setup
Solid setup with good confluence but missing 2 or more key elements. HTF bias is present but perhaps not fully confirmed top-down. Entry is on a valid PDA but may not be the tightest option. Target is visible. Still a tradeable setup but requires more caution on sizing.
HTF Bias Aligned Valid PDA Entry 3-4 Confluences
B
Average Setup
Some confluences present but the overall picture is incomplete. HTF bias may be unclear or entry is not on the preferred PDA. Target exists but is not clean. These trades should be taken at reduced size or avoided during a losing streak. A B-grade during a drawdown is a pass.
2-3 Confluences Unclear HTF Bias Reduce Size
C
Weak Setup
Minimal confluences. Entry is not on a clean PDA, HTF bias is not clearly confirmed, or the target is vague. This is the type of trade typically taken out of boredom or FOMO. Avoid these entirely. If you find yourself taking C-grade trades regularly, step back and review your process.
1-2 Confluences No Clear Target Avoid
D
Do Not Take
No meaningful confluence. This is a random, emotional, or revenge trade. No HTF bias, no clean entry model, no clear target. Taking D-grade trades consistently will destroy an account. If this grade appears in your journal more than once, it is a serious red flag that requires immediate attention.
No Confluences Emotional Entry Do Not Take

Understanding the Journal

Each trade card shows a complete picture of the trade. Here is what each field means and how to read the cards.

Grade Badge
A++ to D — quality of the setup based on ICT + Dodgy iFVG model confluences. See Section 02 above.
Rule Status Badge
✅ Followed all rules · ⚠️ Partially followed · ❌ Rules broken. Assessed manually or via ⚡ Verify.
P&L
Realised profit or loss in USD. For ES: points × $50. For NQ: points × $20. Shown in green (profit) or red (loss).
Confluences
ICT/Dodgy concepts that supported the trade. More high-quality confluences = higher grade. Tags shown on each card.
Entry / Exit Time
Time the trade was entered and exited. Helps identify which part of the session the trade was taken in.
SL / TP
Stop Loss and Take Profit levels. SL preferred above/below IFVG or FVG. TP at a clear liquidity or PDA target.
Notes
General observations about the trade — market context, delivery quality, confluences seen in real time.
Mistakes
What went wrong or could have been better. Reviewed against the rulebook during verification.
Lessons
Key takeaway from the trade. Used to build better habits over time.
⚡ Verify Rules
Runs an AI analysis of the trade against all 13 rules. Shows faults, what was done right, and a final verdict.
Chart
Imgur image shows inline. TradingView link opens in a new tab. Both can be added per trade.
Filters
Filter by session, instrument, direction, grade, rule status, confluences, outcome, and date to study specific scenarios.

Understanding Analytics

The analytics page gives a bird's eye view of performance over time. All data is pulled live from Notion and filtered by the selected time period.

  • Equity Curve — shows cumulative P&L from your first trade to now. A rising curve means you're growing. Use this to spot drawdown periods and recovery patterns.
  • Instruments Traded — donut chart showing what percentage of your trades were on each instrument (NQ, ES etc). Helps identify if you're overexposed to one market.
  • Daily P&L Calendar — each trading day is colour coded. Green = profitable day, red = losing day. Navigate months using the arrows. Use this to spot patterns — are you losing on Mondays? Winning during London?
  • Period Filter — switch between All Time, This Week, This Month, and Last 3 Months to analyse different windows of performance.
  • Stats Bar — total trades, win rate, total P&L, average P&L per trade, and your best single trade.

Understanding the Rules Page

The rules page is the foundation of the journal. Every trade is verified against these 13 rules. Rules are tagged as:

  • Required — non-negotiable. Breaking these is a clear rule violation and will flag as ❌ on a trade.
  • Preferred — best practice. Not breaking them is ideal, but flexibility is allowed with stronger confluences.
  • Work In Progress — acknowledged weaknesses. Tracked but not penalised as harshly in verification.

The rules are based on ICT's methodology and Dodgy's iFVG Ultimate+ model. They exist to create a repeatable, consistent process — not to restrict creativity, but to ensure every trade taken has a logical reason behind it.
